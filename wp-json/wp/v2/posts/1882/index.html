There is no rule in nature. It\u2019s definitely true. You can find many animals out there whose features can shake the world a little. The adorable newborn pup in this story is a great example. This tiny cute guy was born with green fur, so his owners immediately named him Pistachio. Keep scrolling!<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

Recently, an Italian farmer named Cristian Mallocci has welcomed the arrival of five newborn puppies to his home. They were born on a farm located on the Mediterranean island of Sardinia.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

While all four of Pistachio\u2019s siblings had white fur, the cutie had an unusual coat \u2013 green fur. And his name Pistachio reflects this. It matches, right?
\nFor this thing, the green fluffy boy is going to be kept on his farm. Meanwhile, his brothers and sisters will find their new homes. Pistachio will be trained to look after the sheep on the farm with his mother.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

Regarding Pistachio\u2019s green fur, there are two explanations for this.<\/p>\n

The green fur color occurs when a doggy with light-colored fur gets stained by the \u201cmeconium\u201d in the mother\u2019s womb\u2014the earliest stool produced by an infant animal. It can be also caused by a green pigment in the mother\u2019s womb called biliverdin.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

It\u2019s worth mentioning that the green fur of puppies like Pistachio won\u2019t last forever. It\u2019s going to fade until it\u2019s completely gone. But this never affects the doggie. He\u2019s still adorable, smart, and helpful as he used to be.<\/p>\n
