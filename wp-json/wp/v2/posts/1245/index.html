All dogs in the world who live in shelters wish to have a family. But there is a cruel fact that humans adopt pets usually based on their appearances, so the wish of having a forever home seems to take longer for some older dogs who do not have a \u201ccute\u201d look.<\/p>\n

But no matter what every dog deserves love and a forever home, just like this pooch that waited for years to find his family and finally got his wish.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

This is Wally the sweet Pitbull who doesn\u2019t look like your ordinary pooch but he had a rough life and now all that he wanted was some love and care.<\/p>\n

When Wally was brought to the Town of Islip Animal Shelter on Long Island, New York, he was a stray and almost 10 years old.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

AHe was experiencing some health issues including, blindness in one eye and skin infection reports The Dodo. All this gave the poor dog an appearance that worried the shelter staff, they were afraid he never would get adopted.<\/p>\n

The pooch proved to have an amazing personality and became the favorite dog amongst the staff.<\/p>\n

\u201cHe loves to slobber on you and will do anything for some food,\u201d veterinary tech Danielle Gorle told The Dodo. \u201cHe also likes to just hang out and not do anything.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cWally is an amazing walrus\/hippo\/rhino dog that really just needs a home that accepts him and to spend his golden years in!\u201d Jen, a volunteer at the shelter, wrote on Facebook.<\/p>\n

\u201cHe is a little mush and a volunteer favorite.\u201d<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

Many visitors sadly couldn\u2019t see past the dog\u2019s appearance and he had to wait a year to find his home since he also needed to be a one because he doesn\u2019t work well with other animals.<\/p>\n

The shelter staff reports that not getting adopted started to affect his well-being.<\/p>\n

\u201cHe seems to be declining,\u201d said Gorle. \u201cHe was happier when he first came in. He likes when he gets to go out but he seems depressed in his kennel.\u201d<\/p>\n

But thankfully Wally\u2019s story has a happy ending.<\/p>\n

A month after he went viral and we first reported on the dog, the shelter announced that Wally had found his forever home, finally.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

\u201cHe is relaxing comfortably and looks great!\u201d the shelter wrote.<\/p>\n

We are so happy for the sweet furry, every dog deserves love and care.
